Project

Allara Program

A program for women's health

Embark on a journey of discovery tailored uniquely to you, where every question finds its answer, and every doubt is dispelled with personalized precision. At Allara, we understand that your path to well-being is as individual as you are. That's why we invite you to explore a world where clarity meets customization, where the quest for optimal health is met with a symphony of solutions curated just for you. Whether you seek instruction, guidance, or something else, our digital sanctuary awaits, ready to welcome you with open arms and empower you on your path to vitality. In this project, we designed an interactive prototype by using Figma for Allara's website based on the problems they face on their original website. Allara is a company that provides virtual care for women's hormonal and metabolic health.

Based on the project brief provided by our client, we communicated with our client to clarify our understanding of the target customers, problems faced on their original website, and some ideas on the complexity of the final design.

Design Brief

04

Cooperation with real industrial company

During these three weeks of working together, we went from sketch to high-fidelity model. Our goal was not to design the best registration process in the world, but to improve the flaws of the original design as much as possible. Here is how the work was broken down.



01

Week 1: Communication & Sketch

My team members and I first browsed the project summary and communicated our preliminary improvement ideas for the project with the company representative. We then brainstormed several rough sketches of what needed to be redesigned.

02

Week 2: Low-fidelity mockups & Suggestions

After we had a few ideas, we started making low-fidelity mockups based on everyone's sketches. In this process, our main task is to present our ideas and accept suggestions from professors, teaching assistants, and classmates to decide whether these decisions are consistent with the design concept of the project.

03

Week 3: High-fidelity mockups & Reiteration

In the final week, we reworked the suggestions and drew the high-fidelity model. We then recorded the complete process and presented our design. Finally, we presented our design to the person in charge, Molly, in the meeting and took notes on several suggestions from Molly. After the meeting, we made changes to our high-fidelity model based on new suggestions to refine our design process.




User Research

05

Research findings

In order to better complete the redesign, we looked at how to design a good sign up flow for a program and a good user interface. Here are some of my findings:

Designing a good registration process for a program involves creating a seamless and intuitive process that encourages users to register and interact with the platform. Keeping it simple is a key principle to consider when designing your registration flow and user interface. Therefore, we plan to minimize the steps and information required for users to reduce friction and make it easy for them to complete the registration process.

We found that using the idea of progressive disclosure is a good method for us to design the sign up flow for the Allara program. Thus, we decided to break down the sign-up process into manageable steps and present them one at a time. Using progressive disclosure to gradually reveal information and options to users, guiding them through the process without overwhelming them.

Step #1

06

First version of low-fi wireframe

For our first low-fidelity wireframe, we chose designs from our sketches that would best solve the problems the client had with the site. More specifically, we addressed the problem of users potentially being unfamiliar with Allara, uncertain if it suits their needs, and being skeptical about the doctors by changing the flow of the content, and adding narratives as well as interactive elements. In addition, we tried to make choices that would allow personalized and inclusive experiences for the users.


Welcome Page

Treatment History Page

Firstly, we changed the flow of content, using ideas from two of our sketches. In the original version of the site, the screen that checks for user eligibility for the program was provided as the third screen, but we decided to bring this to the first Welcome Page because we thought that this would reduce unnecessary processes for those who are not eligible for the program.

Secondly, we chose the sketch that redesigned the progress bar, where a percentage number was added so that color-blind people could also see their current progress, example shown on Treatment History Page.


Expert Care Page

Medical Care Page

Specialist Page

Thirdly, we used the idea to include an additional page to solve the problem of users not knowing who Allara is and being skeptical of doctors. The purpose of this page was to give a brief introduction to what Allara provides in order to motivate the users to go through the questions and sign up that follow because in the original website, information about Allara was only given near the end of the entire process. In Expert Care Page, we used three buttons that users could click which would lead to a page that would lead to a description of specialists at Allara shown as Specialists Page, a detailed description on the medical care shown as Medical Care Page, and a detailed description on the nutrition support. These buttons were implemented instead of having all the details on one page because we did not want to make the screen text heavy or for users who are familiar with Allara to have to go through the information that they already know. Pictures were added in all of the detail pages to allow a visually intuitive understanding and to break up the text to decrease the density of text.


Previous Diagnoses Page

Join Program Page

Fourthly, we implemented the idea of adding a Previous Diagnoses Page page from one of our sketches. This page was added so that users who click “yes” would be directed to the “diagnosed history” page that was in the original site, while users who click “no” would skip the “diagnosed history” page. The aim of this change was to reduce the amount of text that users who do not have previous diagnoses have to go through and to increase readability.

Fifthly, we also implemented the idea of adding a page after all the questions required before the sign-up has been asked. The aim of Join Program Page was to give a break point in the process where the users are told that they have finished answering the questions and to indicate that what is left is to join and sign up for the program. Providing a brief sentence on how the questions answered so far will be used for a personalized plan when signing up for the program, would motivate the users to join the program.


Uncovered Plan Page

Confirmation Page

Moreover, we used the design decision to make a horizontally swipeable card of the subscription plans with buttons also included at the top to jump to a specific subscription plan to increase readability and to add interactive elements for users, shown on Uncovered Plan Page

Lastly, we implemented the idea to include a Confirmation Page for users at the end of the entire process so that they can check the information they entered and easily confirm that they have successfully joined the program.





Step #2

07

Update Low-fi Wireframe after Critiques

Following the valuable feedback received from our instructor, client, and peers, we proceeded to update our low-fidelity wireframe for Allara Health's onboarding flow. This second iteration incorporates several key changes based on the critiques and suggestions provided.


Specialist Page

Join Program Page

Covered Plans Page

Uncovered Plans Page

We implemented the following changes based on our client's feedback:

  • Added a "continue" button on each page of the specialist, medical care, and nutrition support sections to maintain user engagement within the signup flow
  • Removed the referral question and the hormonal & metabolic evaluation program, as they were no longer relevant
  • Combined bullets 3, 4, and 5 as they all refer to 1:1 visits with providers
  • Removed the community offering
  • Adjusted the progress bar to end at the review page, making the onboarding process appear more manageable

We also made the following changes based on our peers' feedback:

  • Used the expert section from Allara's "About" page to provide descriptions on the specialist page
  • Added more padding to the button text to improve readability
  • Incorporated two guidance dots for swiping or scrolling
  • Featured real-world patient testimonials from Allara Health's website to showcase the program's benefits
  • Added a "read more" and "read less" button for the "Your insurance will be billed for" section on the covered page

However, we decided not to include some suggestions in this low-fidelity iteration:

  • Our instructor's suggestion to enhance the visual design will be addressed in our high-fidelity prototype.
  • The peer feedback regarding adding color, fun elements, and pictures will also be incorporated in the high-fidelity prototype, where we can better explore visual design choices.

By selectively implementing the most relevant and impactful changes in this second iteration, we have created an improved low-fidelity wireframe that better aligns with our client's goals and addresses key user experience concerns raised by our peers.

This iterative process has reinforced the importance of seeking and incorporating feedback from multiple stakeholders to refine and enhance our design solution. As we move forward to the high-fidelity prototyping stage, we will continue to keep the remaining suggestions in mind to create a visually engaging and user-friendly onboarding experience for Allara Health.




Step #3

08

Visual Style Guide

The overall tone remains consistent with the original design, but we have added some button/input box effects, accessibility designs, icons, and card style designs.


For the design of color, font and size, we chose and kept it consistent with the original design to the greatest extent, including all of the text sizes, the effect of mouse hover, the color after being selected, and the original color of the button, etc. For the customer review cards, we also added a three-dot icon for the sliding window and made it the same color as the theme color so that it can blend in with the page.

At the same time, we also made some changes to the original design. For example, on the input box, we added a shadow to the selected, hover button and input box. In this way, users who are not sensitive to color can clearly see the input box they selected.

In the design of the icon, we implemented three blue buttons to increase the interactivity of the page. Users can expand more content by clicking buttons. By browsing this content, users can have a clearer understanding of the product and decide whether this program is suitable for them. In addition, we also added the checked mark for the check box to ensure that color-blind people can also see that they have checked this box by the presentation of the checked mark, not only the color changing in this box. In the same way, we have increased the percentage under the progress bar to better serve the visually impaired.

Finally, for the card and drop-down box designs, we chose to keep the default designs because the client wants us to keep it simple. In addition, we believe that the color of the card is close to the background color of the page, which may not be conducive to people with visual impairments. Therefore, we recommend the client to consider using other colors to design cards.

Lessons Learned

11

Reflecting on the project outcomes

This was a quick project, lasting only three weeks from start to submission. Here are my reflections:

01

Collaboration Skills

I believe that the purpose of group work is not just to complete the task. Group work teaches valuable collaboration skills, including communication, teamwork, and conflict resolution. Working with group members requires effective communication to share ideas, delegate tasks, and coordinate efforts to achieve common goals.

02

Time Management

When working in a group, don't put off all tasks until the end. While working with my teammates, I also learned time management techniques, as well as the skills of setting deadlines and allocating resources to complete tasks within the designated time. In fact, effective time management is crucial to meeting project deadlines and achieving goals. Otherwise, it may bring great psychological pressure to myself or my teammates.
