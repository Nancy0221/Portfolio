Project

01

Responsive Redesign

A food ordering website with good responsive design

As we all know, with the development of technology, many restaurants have designed their own web pages to support online ordering. Users may place an order on a computer, or on a mobile phone or tablet. Therefore, it is very important for the website to have a good responsive design. As a skilled designer and developer, I leverage Figma to bring concepts to life with precision and elegance. From wireframes to high-fidelity designs, Figma serves as my canvas for captivating crafting user experiences. In addition, my skills of HTML/CSS enable me to translate designs into responsive, pixel-perfect websites. Next, I will show how I used these two techniques to design a better solution.

Design Brief

04

From conception to design & coded prototype in 2 weeks

I'm proud of this project because it only took me two weeks from conception to coding. The completed results also met expectations, that is, the problems we previously found in the original website were improved. Here is how the work was broken down.



01

Week 1: Finding Problems & Design

I identified some visual design and accessibility issues with the website, and then started sketching out the areas I wanted to redesign for the site, finally creating a low-fidelity mockup of the site.

02

Week 2: Design & Code

I designed a high-fidelity model based on the low-fidelity model from the previous week. Then code it using HTML and CSS based on the high-fidelity mockup. During the coding process, I appropriately adjusted design issues that I had not noticed when designing the high-fidelity mockup, such as making the structure of the website understandable to blind readers.




User Research

05

Research findings

In order to better complete the redesign, I checked several blogs and papers about interface design. Here are some of my findings:

With the increasing use of smartphones and tablets for browsing the internet, the papers that I read highlights the importance of optimizing websites for mobile devices. Studies show that a significant portion of web traffic comes from mobile devices, underscoring the need for responsive design to accommodate mobile users effectively.

Responsive design can have a positive impact on conversion rates by providing a consistent and user-friendly experience across devices. Research suggests that users are more likely to complete desired actions, such as making a purchase or filling out a form, on websites that are optimized for their device.

Lessons Learned

14

Reflecting on the project outcomes

This was a quick project, lasting only two weeks from start to submission. Here are my reflections:

01

Providing good feedback

It is not enough to say that the design is wrong. It would be more helpful to describe why and what might be done, because that can provide direction.

02

Having a goal of design

Sometimes I find that adding accessibility features will reduce the aesthetics of the website. Therefore, when designing the website, I must consider what the goal of designing the website is. Is it for aesthetics or ease of use? Or both?
