Employee Information System

A website where you can filter, sort, and select employees by criteria

Imagine this: You're a busy boss overseeing a thriving company with a diverse team of talented individuals. With this Employee Information System, you have the power to effortlessly navigate through your employee database, making strategic decisions that drive growth and success. Upon landing on the homepage, you're greeted with a sleek interface that allows you to filter employees by job title and gender, giving you instant access to the information you need. Want to find all female software engineers? No problem. Looking for male engineers? Done.

But I don't stop there. I understand that as the boss, you need to make informed decisions about promotions and salary increases. That's why I've implemented an innovative feature that allows you to handpick the employees you believe deserve recognition and reward. Simply select the individuals you want to promote, and their names, positions, and salaries will be displayed prominently, putting their achievements in the spotlight. In addition, I've included a real-time counter that shows you the total number of selected employees, ensuring that you always have a clear understanding of your decisions' impact. But what if you change your mind or want to start fresh? No problem. With just a click of a button, you can reset all search conditions, giving you the freedom to explore new possibilities without any hassle.

Design Brief

04

Welcome to the Employee Information System, where efficiency meets empowerment!

This project provides a comprehensive solution for businesses to manage their workforce with ease and flexibility. Here is how the work was broken down.



01

Week 1: Research & Design

I conduct research on product features at Google, Amazon, and YouTube. Analyze the results of the survey and make a preliminary plan for what functions the website I design will have. For example, I think that Google bookmarks follows the design principle of simplicity. Its interface is clean and simple, focusing on the core functionality of bookmarking web pages without unnecessary clutter. Furthermore, in YouTube, as the number of videos in the playlist increases, browsing them can become challenging. For my design, I hope that simplicity can be integrated into the interface I design, so that the page can display the content aggregated by users concisely and clearly. In addition, in my development, I hope that I can display the user's search content through the filtering function, thereby improving usage efficiency.

02

Week 2: Code & Test

Once I decided what functionality I wanted to implement and the general structure of the site, I started coding using HTML/CSS and JavaScript, and finally test it. In fact, the testing process was not smooth sailing, but I used the built-in debugger of VS Code and the browser's inspection function to solve the difficulties I encountered.
